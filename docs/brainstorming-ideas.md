## The Dvořák Vision

Antonín Dvořák arrived in America in 1892 — an outsider, a synthesizer. He heard spirituals and Native American music and wrote it into Symphony No. 9 "From the New World" before most Americans recognized what they had. "Going Home" became universal because he saw across boundaries others couldn't.

**The parallel to SPL/Momagrid:**
- Dvořák didn't theorize about American music — he wrote the symphony
- We didn't theorize about declarative AI workflows — we built the engine (48 hours, working prototype)
- The fear of AI today mirrors fear of every transformative wave: printing press, electricity, internet
- Always the same argument — jobs vanish, human meaning erodes
- Sometimes partially true short-term. Always wrong about the long arc.

## Multi-Modal Vision (future SPL/Momagrid)

*This is where the platform goes next — raw ideas only, implementation TBD*

- **Multi-modal input**: voice, image, video, document scan → SPL workflow
- **Multi-modal output**: text → music generation, text → image, text → story narration (TTS)
- **The baby use case**: a child speaks to the app → SPL workflow processes the request → exciting music or bedtime story read back in a warm voice
  - This is the ultimate accessibility test: if a baby can use it, the barrier is truly zero
  - Shows the platform is not just for engineers — it's for everyone
- **SPL as the orchestration layer**: the `.spl` file doesn't care whether the input is text or voice — the adapter handles modality translation
- **Momagrid nodes serving multi-modal inference**: phone NPUs already do on-device speech recognition and TTS — they become natural multi-modal nodes
- **Opportunities**:
  - Education: children's interactive storytelling
  - Accessibility: voice-first interfaces for non-literate users
  - Healthcare: patient describes symptoms in natural language → SPL triage workflow
  - Creative: musician hums a melody → SPL workflow generates sheet music + arrangement
  - Language learning: speak in your native language → SPL translates + responds in target language with audio
